EMR - ELECTRONIC MEDICAL RECORDS

Electronic medical record (EMR) is a digital version of all the information you would normally find in medical records and related documents of a medical facility such as medical history, medical history, diagnosis diagnosis, medications, vaccination dates, allergy history, test results, and doctor's notes.

Who use EMR commonly?

EMR refers to medical records that are privately owned by a particular medical facility, be it a hospital, polyclinic, specialty clinic. EMR includes information collected by physicians to diagnose and treat their patients, which is recorded for internal use, and this information cannot be used outside of that medical facility.

Why EMR?

Information is not only provided quickly but it is also updated accurately and regularly, thereby reducing medical information errors and improving patient care.

Detailer and clearer patient records.

Reducing duplicate information hence saving time and money for patients and medical facilities.

Improving accessibility to information, which makes writing prescriptions safer and more reliable.

Promoting patient engagement, which can encourage healthy living and the more frequent use of preventive measures.

Making the diagnosis more accurate.
